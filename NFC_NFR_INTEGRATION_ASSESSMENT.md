# NFR Integration Assessment

*Produced L+44 on operator request. An honest evaluation of Nested
Fibrational Realization (NFR) against the corpus's own promotion and
legitimacy criteria (Book VII promotion law; Book V branch legitimacy;
Book VII import discipline), to determine whether NFR has matured toward
canonical promotion or whether its role is otherwise. Assessment only:
no obligation status changed, no promotion enacted.*

**Verdict in one line:** NFR is **not promotable** — to branch or to
canonical content — and its promotion would violate both the promotion
law and branch legitimacy; but its defect ledgers have matured into a
**legitimate cross-branch tracking instrument** the obligation roster
may reference under import discipline, without promotion.

---

## 1. Branch promotion — assessed against Book V legitimacy

**def:legitimacy-witness** requires (1) the endpoint datum arises by
certified descent from **U** through a declared realization functor F;
(2) branch-visibility (depends only on **U** and F); (3) no undeclared
supplementary structure.

NFR's endpoint E_NFR^dist1 is built from **admitted YM/GR endpoint data
and parent residues** (X_YM, Y_GR, P_YM, Q_GR, V_NF2, M_SM^3,
M_SM^K4,struct) — it is a bridge *over already-certified branch
outputs*, not a fresh certified descent from **U** through a functor.
It therefore does not satisfy (1): there is no F : **U** → E_NFR;
NFR sits downstream of the branches whose endpoints it organizes.

**NFR self-disclaims this directly:** its non-claims list includes "full
branch legitimacy." **Result: fails branch legitimacy. Not branch-promotable.**

## 2. Canonical-content promotion — assessed against Book VII promotion law

**thm:promotion-requires-transfer** requires both (1) a soundly scoped
certificate of the result on its declared regime, and (2) a separately
proved transfer theorem carrying it to the promoted scope. Neither alone
suffices.

NFR holds **AUDIT-R^dist1** — a scoped certificate that NFR passed
restricted audit *at structurally matter-enlarged restricted scope*.
That satisfies (1) for the claim "NFR is a sound restricted bridge tool."
It does **not** satisfy (2): there is no transfer theorem carrying any
NFR content to canonical (branch/full) scope, and NFR explicitly is
"not harmonization closure." Moreover NFR's own positive claims (AUDIT-R,
TOOL-R, DIST-R, FIREWALL-R) are about the *tool's restricted-scope
soundness*, not about discharging any canonical obligation: TOOL-R states
the four top-level defects are "sharpened into constrained ledgers —
sharpened, not discharged," and "all four top-level defects remain
active." **Result: no promotable canonical content. Not content-promotable.**

## 3. Legitimate role — assessed against Book VII import discipline

**def:admissible-import:** an import cited with status, scope, and
dependency intact, not promoted beyond that status.

This is where NFR has a genuine, mature role. Its defect ledgers are a
*status-preserving localization* of obligations already tracked in the
branches:
- **d_scp^YM,C = (d_ID^P, d_RIG^C, d_ENC^C, d_GLOB^C, d_CLU^C)** maps
  one-to-one onto YM's obligations O-ID / O-RIG / O-ENC / O-GLOB / O-CLU
  (all five verified present in the YM branch).
- **d_scp^GR,B = (d_real^B, d_deform^B, d_compat^B, d_closure^B,
  d_extension^B)** maps onto the GR bridge-stack obligations (GR
  deform/closure/extension verified present; def:curv-subcrit-global
  is the global-stability frontier marker).
- **d_intf^C** localizes the YM/GR interface defect into (type, functor,
  compatibility, regime/scope).
- **d_mat^K4** is the SM matter-completion defect (reduced-frontier
  Item 3), as a constrained ledger.

NFR's witness discipline is exactly import discipline: w_src
(declared-input), w_nh (no hidden supplementation), w_scp
(inherited-scope preservation). It imports branch endpoint data with
status intact and promotes nothing. **Result: NFR is a well-formed
admissible-import organizer — a legitimate cross-branch defect-localization
instrument, usable as a roster cross-reference without promotion.**

## 4. The structural K4 datum — bounded and firewalled

M_SM^K4,struct → T_NFR^dist (the finite Klein-four 1+2 generation-position
distinction) is the one genuinely new datum NFR introduces. It is
**DIST-R: lawfully nonempty at restricted structural scope only**, under
the load-bearing w_dist firewall: any phenomenological, mass-hierarchy,
matter-completion, Hopf/E8, or source-ontology use sets w_dist = 0 and
voids the assignment. It is **not promotable** — promotion would assert
exactly the matter completion the firewall forbids. Recorded as a
firewalled structural observation; nothing more is claimed or claimable.

---

## 5. Conclusion and disposition

- **Promotion-readiness: NO**, on two independent grounds (branch
  legitimacy §1; promotion law §2), each confirmed by NFR's own
  self-classification. NFR correctly remains a restricted bridge tool in
  the Speculative Holding register. Promoting it would be a status
  inflation prohibited by Book VII.
- **Mature legitimate role: YES**, as a cross-branch defect-localization
  instrument under import discipline (§3). The obligation roster may
  reference NFR's ledgers to show how the YM/GR/SM scope obligations
  localize and interrelate — a tracking convenience, not a promotion.
- **The K4 datum** stays a firewalled structural observation (§4).

**Net:** the operator's flag is correctly answered. NFR has *not* matured
toward promotion — and its own architecture is designed so it cannot,
which is its integrity, not its limitation. What it offers the canon is a
disciplined cross-branch *view* of the residual frontier (the YM/GR scope
residues, their interface, and the matter defect, as one constrained
ledger), which the roster now cross-references. The L+42/L+43 feasibility
verdict is unaffected: NFR sharpens the cartography of the toolkit
boundary; it does not cross it.

*Assessment only. NFR status unchanged: AUDIT-R^dist1 + TOOL-R + DIST-R +
FIREWALL-R, restricted bridge tool, Speculative Holding register.*
