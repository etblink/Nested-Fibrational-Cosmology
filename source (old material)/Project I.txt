## I.16.3 Statement

Assume the protected/bath coupled system has the standard form
[

  [

  on the protected band (same (\kappa) as before, no new small parameters).

## I.16.3.A — Leakage bound (bath amplitude stays (O(\delta)))

Duhamel gives
[
\eta(t)=e^{Lt}\eta(0)+\int_0^t e^{L(t-s)},C(s)x(s),ds.
]
Thus
[
|\eta(t)|
\le e^{-\gamma t}|\eta(0)|+\int_0^t e^{-\gamma(t-s)},\kappa,\delta(s),|x(s)|,ds.
]
Since the baseline (A(t))-flow is unitary and the correction is quadratic (below), we may take (|x(s)|\approx 1) to leading order, yielding the clean bound
[
\boxed{\ |\eta(t)|\ \le\ e^{-\gamma t}|\eta(0)|+\kappa\int_0^t e^{-\gamma(t-s)}\delta(s),ds\ }
]
and in particular
[
\boxed{\ \sup_{t\ge0}|\eta(t)|\ \le\ |\eta(0)|+\frac{\kappa}{\gamma},\sup_{s\ge0}\delta(s)\ }.
]

## I.16.3.B — Protected drift is (O(\delta^2)) (integrated)

Let (U_A(t,0)) be the unitary propagator generated by (A(t)). Then
[
x(t)=U_A(t,0)x(0)+\int_0^t U_A(t,s),B(s)\eta(s),ds,
]
so
[
|x(t)-U_A(t,0)x(0)|
\le\int_0^t |B(s)|,|\eta(s)|,ds.
]
Insert (|B(s)|\le \kappa\delta(s)) and the leakage bound above to get the decisive scaling:
[
\boxed{\ |x(t)-U_A(t,0)x(0)|\ \lesssim\ \int_0^t \delta(s)\Bigl(e^{-\gamma s}|\eta(0)|+\kappa!!\int_0^s e^{-\gamma(s-u)}\delta(u),du\Bigr),ds\ }.
]

In the common “clean start” case (\eta(0)=0), this collapses to a genuinely quadratic control:
[
\boxed{\ |x(t)-U_A(t,0)x(0)|\ \le\ \frac{\kappa^2}{\gamma}\int_0^t \delta(s)^2,ds\ }.
]

### Corollary (variable-defect stability timescale)

then the protected evolution remains close to the unitary baseline and the back-reaction remains small—this is the non-autonomous analogue of (t\ll \gamma/(\kappa\delta)^2) when (\delta) is constant.
