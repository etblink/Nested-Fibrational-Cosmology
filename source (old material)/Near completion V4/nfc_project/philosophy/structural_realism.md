# NFC and Structural Realism
## A Philosophical Analysis

**Status:** Philosophical investigation | **Scope:** Metaphysics of science | **Dependencies:** Full NFC corpus

---

## Preamble: What is Structural Realism?

**Structural Realism (SR)** is the position that:
- Scientific theories describe **structure**, not **objects**
- Structure is what survives theory change
- Structure is what we can know

**Varieties**:
- **Epistemic SR (ESR)**: We can only know structure, not nature of objects
- **Ontic SR (OSR)**: Structure is all there is — no objects

**Question**: Where does NFC fit?

---

## 1. NFC as Radical Ontic Structural Realism

### 1.1 The NFC Position

NFC asserts:
- **No primitive objects**: Relations are primary
- **No primitive identity**: Identity is derived compression
- **No intrinsic properties**: All properties are relational

This is **stronger** than standard OSR.

### 1.2 Comparison with Ladyman & Ross

| Ladyman & Ross OSR | NFC |
|-------------------|-----|
| Relations are primary | ✅ Same |
| Objects are "placeholders" | ✅ Same |
| Identity is structural | ✅ Same |
| No primitive individuals | ✅ Same |
| **Pattern realism** | **Compression realism** |

**Key difference**: NFC replaces "patterns" with "compression classes" — more precise, mathematically grounded.

## 2. The Identity Problem in SR

### 2.1 The Challenge

Standard OSR struggles with:
- **Leibniz's Principle**: If structure is all, what distinguishes isomorphic structures?
- **Haecceity**: Do objects have "thisness" beyond structure?
- **Re-identification**: How do we track objects across time?

### 2.2 NFC's Solution

**Theorem (NFC Identity)**: Identity is **derived**, not primitive.

**Mechanism**:
1. Relational configurations are tokens (no identity)
2. Finite distinguishability forces compression
3. Stable compression classes under extension = derived identity
4. Identity is **contextual, revisable, finite**

**Philosophical payoff**:
- No need for haecceity
- No need for primitive identity
- Re-identification = stability under extension

## 3. Theory Change and Structural Continuity

### 3.1 The Pessimistic Meta-Induction

**Problem**: Scientific theories change; why believe current theory is true?

**Standard SR response**: Structure survives theory change.

### 3.2 NFC's Response

**NFC claim**: What survives is **compression-stable structure**.

**Mechanism**:
- Old theory: certain compression classes
- New theory: refined compression classes
- Surviving structure = stable under refinement

**Example**: Classical mechanics → Quantum mechanics
- Classical objects = coarse compression classes
- Quantum states = fine compression classes
- Surviving structure = conservation laws (stabilizers)

## 4. The No-Miracles Argument

### 4.1 The Argument

**Premise**: Success of science would be miraculous if theories were not approximately true.

**Conclusion**: Scientific theories are approximately true.

### 4.2 NFC's Reformulation

**NFC premise**: Success of science would be miraculous if compression-stable structure did not track reality.

**NFC conclusion**: Compression-stable structure tracks reality.

**Key difference**: NFC doesn't claim theories are "true" — claims compression-stable structure is **reliable**.

## 5. Scientific Representation

### 5.1 The Problem

How do scientific models represent reality?

**Standard views**:
- **Isomorphism**: Model and reality share structure
- **Similarity**: Model is similar to reality
- **Fiction**: Models are useful fictions

### 5.2 NFC's View

**NFC claim**: Representation is **compression**.

**Mechanism**:
- Reality: full relational structure
- Model: compressed structure (finite distinguishability)
- Representation = witness-induced compression

**Philosophical payoff**:
- No need for isomorphism (too strong)
- No need for similarity (vague)
- No need for fiction (anti-realist)

## 6. Objections and Replies

### 6.1 Objection: Structure Needs Objects

**Objection**: Relations need relata. NFC's "pure relations" are incoherent.

**Reply**: NFC's relations are **tokens**, not relata-bearing. They have incidence (where they connect) without identity (what they are).

**Analogy**: Graph edges have endpoints without needing "edge identity" across graphs.

---

### 6.2 Objection: No Causal Power

**Objection**: Structure without objects has no causal power.

**Reply**: NFC has **defect-mediated interaction** — causal structure without object-causation.

**Analogy**: Network effects emerge from topology, not node properties.

---

### 6.3 Objection: Not Empirically Testable

**Objection**: NFC is too abstract to be tested.

**Reply**: NFC is **foundational**, not phenomenological. Testability comes via:
- Physical instantiations (LQG, CST)
- Structural constraints on theories
- Computational verification

## 7. NFC as Methodology

### 7.1 The NFC Method

1. **Start minimal**: Fewest primitives
2. **Derive structure**: What must exist?
3. **Flag assumptions**: What is not derived?
4. **Audit claims**: Do they match demonstration?

### 7.2 Application to Scientific Practice

**Example**: Developing a new theory
- What are the minimal ontological commitments?
- What structure is derived vs. assumed?
- Is the theory NFC-compatible?

**Benefit**: Disciplines theory-building, prevents smuggling.
