items of the reduced irreducible frontier (Book VII; verified against
the obligation roster L+41) are reachable with current canonical NFC
machinery ("in-toolkit"), or require machinery the corpus does not

in-toolkit for unconditional discharge. The reduced frontier *is* the
toolkit boundary. This is the expected and correct outcome for a
framework that has honestly reduced famously-hard problems to their
irreducible cores and stopped there, rather than manufacturing a
resolution. Each item is characterized below by the specific external
machinery it would require.

## Item 1 — RH arithmetic orbit grammar (first hard block)

**Current state:** conditionally cleared for the scaling-flow candidate
(cor:rh-sf-first-hard-block-cleared). The log-derivative legality and
trace-pairing sub-obligations are conditionally discharged; the live
residual is ob:rh-sf-d2-audit and the first deep hinge.

**Deepest hinge (cor:rh-f4-1, item 1):** *uniform arithmetic descent*
Ξ → K_ρ → A_ρ — the passage from the canonical object to arithmetic-
scaling data.

**Feasibility: NOT in-toolkit.** The corpus states this directly: the
hinge reduces to whether Q[ψ] ≥ 0 can be proved from the arithmetic
structure of Ξ *without assuming RH*, and the branch records this as
"an irreducible observational information question — [a] deepest
frontier ... with no NFC collar-algebra tool available to resolve [it]
directly." The remaining content is genuinely arithmetic (prime-power
trace data); NFC's observational/collar-algebra apparatus certifies
what admissible probes can see, and this hinge asks for an arithmetic
positivity fact that lies outside what observational data alone can
supply. **External machinery required:** an arithmetic positivity/
descent input about Ξ — essentially the analytic-number-theoretic core
of RH itself.
