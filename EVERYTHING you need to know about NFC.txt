## Methods: Incorporation of External Knowledge

This framework makes extensive contact with established results in mathematics and physics. However, no external structure is assumed as primitive, generative, or ontological.

External knowledge is incorporated **exclusively** in one of the following four roles:

### 1. Classification Constraints

Known classification theorems are used to **identify uniqueness** once an internal structure has been specified by invariant properties (e.g., dimension, simplicity, rank). No classification result is used to posit the existence of a structure.

### 2. Impossibility Boundaries

Established no-go theorems are used to **exclude alternative branches** of the construction. These results function as boundary walls, not scaffolding.

### 3. Survivor Filters

When multiple internally admissible structures exist, external knowledge is used only to identify which are **known to be stable** under the imposed constraints. Structures failing these filters are discarded, not modified.

### 4. Falsification Thresholds

Empirical data enter solely as **numerical tolerance bounds**. Agreement within tolerance does not confirm the framework; violation beyond tolerance falsifies it.

At no point is an external object (e.g., a Lie group, manifold, algebra, or physical model) assumed as a starting point. When an internally derived structure matches a known object, the identification is used only for orientation and terminology; all logical dependence remains internal and equation-anchored.

This methodological discipline is enforced by a global brittleness principle: violation of any core constraint results in rejection of the entire framework rather than local repair.
