A theorem carrying a stale label (declared status exceeds effective status) must be corrected to its effective status before it may be cited at the declared force. Proof: by prop:VII.cite-declared-force, a theorem may be cited only with the force licensed by its declared dependencies. If the declared status exceeds the effective status, the declared force exceeds what the dependency closure licenses. Therefore citation at declared force would constitute citation beyond licensed force. Correction to effective status is the necessary and sufficient remedy.
