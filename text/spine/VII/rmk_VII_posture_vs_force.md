Two distinct vocabularies govern the canon's governance layer and must not be conflated. Claim-force labels ([U], [C], [B], [O], [D], [R]) are theorem-level designators that attach to individual theorem-like objects and record the logical force of each object's proof. Branch-posture labels (CERT-PROJ, conditional CERT-CLOSE, domain-bounded CERT-CLOSE, CERT-CLOSE, frontier-blocked, intrinsic-structural-close, inherited-scope-open) are summary characterizations of a branch's collective achievement and describe the aggregate standing of a branch's claim graph, not the force of any individual theorem. A branch at conditional CERT-CLOSE posture may contain theorems carrying [U], [C], and [O] labels simultaneously. A branch whose posture summary lags behind the actual claim graph carries posture drift, which is distinct from stale-label drift (per-claim mislabeling) and requires explicit correction.
