A theorem may be cited only with the force licensed by its declared status and dependencies. If the declared status is [C], the citing work inherits all the hypotheses on which [C] depends. If the declared status is [O], the theorem may not be cited as establishing any positive conclusion. A [U] result may be cited unconditionally. This proposition is the formal ground for the No-Inflation Standing Rule: the citing party cannot enlarge the force of a result beyond its declared status.
