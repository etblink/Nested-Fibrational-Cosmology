The Discharge Mode Taxonomy identifies four modes by which an obligation may be discharged: (i) Full discharge — the theorem establishes the complete burden at the declared scope; the obligation is closed. (ii) Partial discharge — the theorem establishes a proper sub-burden; the obligation remains open; partial discharge records progress but does NOT close the obligation. (iii) Scoped discharge — the theorem discharges on a declared sub-regime strictly smaller than the obligation's stated scope; the obligation remains open at the wider scope. (iv) Superseding discharge — the theorem replaces the obligation with a reformulated obligation of equivalent or narrower burden; the original is retired; the successor is opened. In every mode, the discharge must be recorded as an explicit edge from the theorem to the obligation. Proximity to stronger results, renaming, or prose softening does not constitute discharge in any mode.
