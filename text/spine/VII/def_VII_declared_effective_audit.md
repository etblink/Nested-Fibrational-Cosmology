Three status quantities attach to every theorem-like object in the canon. Declared status is the force tag ([U], [C], [B], [O], [D], or [R]) explicitly annotated at the point of insertion. Effective status is the force actually licensed by the dependency closure of the proof: if any dependency carries force weaker than [U], the effective status is at most the weakest force in the closure. Effective status is computed from the dependency graph, not hand-annotated. Audit status is the agreement or mismatch between declared and effective status. A theorem whose declared status exceeds its effective status carries a stale label. Stale labels must be corrected before the theorem may be cited at its declared force.
