Promotion Requires Transfer Theorem: A conditional result may not be promoted to an unconditional result except through an explicit Transfer Theorem that discharges all remaining hypotheses. The Transfer Theorem must provide an unconditional proof of each hypothesis of the conditional result, or reduce those hypotheses to established unconditional results. Consequence: the common pattern of "conditional result + informal argument" does not yield an unconditional result; only a certified Transfer Theorem does.
