The transport invariant I_nl(R, U) is the canonical invariant associated to a regime R and a POT object U. It is the witness that source structure is correctly transported under POT-morphisms. The invariant is: (a) witness-descending under the observational quotient; (b) extension-stable on admissible enlargements; (c) normalization-compatible. These three properties are proved unconditionally in thm:IV.normalization. The transport invariant is the formal instrument for verifying that fiber data from different regimes is consistently assembled.
