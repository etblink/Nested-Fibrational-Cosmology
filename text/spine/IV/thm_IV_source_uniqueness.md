Certified Uniqueness of the Universal Source: any two universal source objects (any two POT objects satisfying the universal factorization property) are related by a unique POT-isomorphism. This is Thm. IV.5.1(e) restated to emphasize uniqueness at the source level. The uniqueness is by factorization role, not by presentation: two presentations that differ in presentation-level surplus are still source-equivalent and hence indistinct at the canonical level. This uniqueness is what makes the phrase "the universal source" valid rather than "a universal source."
