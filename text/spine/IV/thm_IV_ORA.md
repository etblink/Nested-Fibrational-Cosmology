Overlap Route-Agreement Theorem (ORA): for any compatible overlap (a common source Q with enlargements to R and R'), there exists a unique canonical POT-isomorphism theta_{R,R';Q} compatible with all fiber components (Sigma, Lambda, T, D). Proof: the isomorphism is constructed from the reindexing functors restricted to the common source Q. Route-independence follows because fiber data is defined canonically from the admissible-test structure, with no ambiguity depending on which enlargement path was used. The ORA is the descent seed for the POT fibration: it proves that two different ways of looking at data from the same source Q give canonically equivalent results.
