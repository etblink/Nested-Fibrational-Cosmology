The candidate universal source is defined as U = T / ~_pres, the quotient of the total fiber T by the presentation equivalence relation ~_pres. Intuitively: the total fiber T collects all fiber data from all regimes; presentation equivalence identifies data that differ only in presentation-level surplus (as defined by cor:I.no-presentation-surplus); the quotient removes this surplus. The candidate U is a POT object (proved in lem:IV.candidate-POT-object). The claim that this candidate satisfies the universal factorization property is proved in thm:IV.universal-completion.
