A legitimacy witness for a branch candidate (C, F) is a formal certificate that the endpoint datum E = F(U) depends only on: the universal source U, the declared realization functor F, and the declared branch-specific hypotheses. The witness is the canonical proof object that certifies no hidden channels are present. It is required for UBLT condition 4. A branch without a legitimacy witness cannot claim canonical endpoint force, regardless of how plausible its endpoint datum might be.
