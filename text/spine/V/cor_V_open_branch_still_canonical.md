Open Branch Still Canonical: a branch with open obligations is still lawful under the UBLT. Proof: the five UBLT conditions concern the structure of the branch (licensed intake, source descent, endpoint visibility, legitimacy witness, no hidden channels) — not the completeness of the branch (all obligations discharged). A branch that honestly records its open obligations has declared them; the legitimate witness covers the declared structure, not the completeness of the closed obligations. This corollary is crucial: it establishes that honesty about open frontiers is compatible with canonical legitimacy. NS, RH, YM, and GR all have open obligations and are all lawful branches.
