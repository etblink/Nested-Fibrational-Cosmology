Collapse Criterion: if two proposed descendants of U are path-equivalent and endpoint-equivalent, they do not constitute distinct lawful branches. Proof: path-equivalence means the certified descent routes are the same. Endpoint-equivalence means the terminal descendant structures are the same up to the target category's native equivalence. By sr:distinctness-by-endpoint, two branches are distinct only to the extent that certified endpoint-visible structure distinguishes them. Since neither path nor endpoint distinguishes the two proposed descendants, they are canonically the same branch. The Collapse Criterion prevents branch inflation by redescription: calling the same descent path by a new name does not create a new branch.
