A hidden channel is any structural input to the endpoint datum E that is not visible in the declared legitimacy witness. Equivalently: E depends on a hidden channel if there exists a structural parameter p such that varying p changes E, but p does not appear in the declared source descent, declared realization functor, or declared branch-specific hypotheses. Hidden channels are the canonical smuggling mechanism for branches: they allow undeclared structure to influence the endpoint without appearing in the record. By prop:V.hidden-channel-exclusion, any branch depending on a hidden channel is non-canonical.
