Diamond Confluence Principle: if two lawful descent paths from U into a declared target category are endpoint-equivalent, they are canonically confluent at the endpoint, even when they remain path-distinct. Proof: endpoint-equivalence identifies the terminal descendant structure up to the target category's native equivalence. Path-distinctness records that the certified descent routes are different. Both facts can hold simultaneously: the endpoint-equivalence does not force the routes themselves to coincide. Therefore confluence at the endpoint does not require collapse of the routes. Together with the Collapse Criterion: Book V permits lawful multiplicity (distinct routes to a common endpoint) while forbidding arbitrary branch inflation (calling the same thing new by redescription).
