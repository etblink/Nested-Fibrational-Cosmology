A diamond confluence configuration is a pair of lawful descent paths from U into the same licensed target category whose endpoint data agree up to the native equivalence of that category. The diamond name reflects the shape: two paths from a common source converging at a common endpoint, while remaining path-distinct in between. Diamond confluence is the canonical representation of lawful multiplicity: distinct routes to a common endpoint are allowed and meaningful, unlike collapse (path-equivalent routes that are the same branch) or inflation (calling the same endpoint new by redescription).
