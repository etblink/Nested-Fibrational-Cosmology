Branch Projection Theorem (Prop. V.3.1): for every licensed branch candidate (C, F), the endpoint datum E = F(U) is well-defined in C and canonical — it depends only on U and the declared realization functor F. Proof: U exists and is unique up to unique POT-isomorphism (Book IV, Thm IV.4.1 and IV.5.1). A realization functor F maps POT-isomorphisms to isomorphisms in C. Therefore E = F(U) is well-defined up to isomorphism in C. This is the canonical existence result for branch endpoints: every licensed branch candidate has a well-defined, canonical endpoint datum.
