Book II has a two-layer structure: [U] layer and [C] layer.

[U] layer (unconditional): DW from A2, TC from standing rule, covering bound for K_0=7, LS-2 universal for K_0=7 Phase-A substrates, PASS as derived necessity, binary rigidity, local nuclear theorem.

[C] layer (conditional on LS-2 and/or Phase-A): LS-2 hypothesis itself, ls2-from-conditions (rho*=2 under DW+TC+LAR), boundary determinacy, boundary capacity counting bound, SCT.1 through SCT.6b coercive transport doctrine, governing theorem.

LS-2 is the principal conditional hinge. For canonical K_0=7 Phase-A substrates, LS-2 holds universally (cor:II.ls2-universal-K07 [U]), so the conditional layer becomes effectively unconditional in the canonical case. In the general case, LS-2 remains a named conditional hypothesis that branches must verify.

This two-layer structure is the governance architecture of Book II: the [U] layer establishes what holds without any locality assumption; the [C] layer shows what follows once locality is established.
