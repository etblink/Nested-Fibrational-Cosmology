Boundary Stabilization Theorem (Book II Governing): under the declared local structural conditions (LS-2 + Phase-A + K_0=7), three claims hold: (1) stabilized local outcome is determined by stabilized collar data (from thm:II.boundary-det); (2) distinct stabilized outcomes inject into stabilized collar types; (3) multiplicity of stabilized outcomes is bounded by boundary capacity (thm:II.boundary-capacity). Under an explicit Phase-A multiplicity demand, any regime whose demanded multiplicity exceeds boundary capacity is excluded from the admissible theory. This theorem is conditional [C]: it requires LS-2 and Phase-A. The unconditional foundation (PASS, binary rigidity, local-nuclear) does not require these hypotheses.
