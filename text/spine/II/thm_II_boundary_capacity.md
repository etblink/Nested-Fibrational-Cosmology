Boundary Capacity Counting Bound: under LS-2 with stabilized collar alphabet of size K_0, the number of distinct stabilized local outcomes on any finite region U is bounded by the boundary capacity: |{realized outcomes on U}| <= K_0^|del_rho*(U)|. For K_0=7 this gives a concrete finite bound. The capacity bound is the technical foundation of the Phase-A exclusion argument: if a demanded multiplicity exceeds boundary capacity, the regime is excluded.
