Conserved charges are persistent observables that are exactly preserved under all admissible transfer maps: phi(Q(omega)) = Q(phi(omega)) for all omega and all admissible transfer maps phi. They are the canonical notion of a conservation law in Book III: a conserved charge is one that survives transfer with zero defect contribution. The charge conservation lemma (lem:III.charge-conservation) establishes that conserved charges have zero contribution to the coercive-transport inequality's E_n term.
