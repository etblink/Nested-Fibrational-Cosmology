The interface-relative stability functional C_n(U_n) is the canonical functional measuring how much certified content survives at the boundary of the region U_n. It tracks: (1) the transported bulk — certified content from U_{n-1} that survives enlargement; (2) the internal defect — defect contributions from interior sites during enlargement; (3) the boundary defect — defect contributions from the boundary del(U_n) via the LS-2 boundary law. The functional C_n is the primary quantity controlled by the Unified Coercive-Transport Inequality (thm:III.unified-coercive).
