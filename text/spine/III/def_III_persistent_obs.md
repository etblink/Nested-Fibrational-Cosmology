A persistent observable is an observable that is (1) quotient-visible: invariant under the observational quotient ~_n at each stage (sr:III.quotient-visible); and (2) transfer-stable: preserved under admissible context refinements up to the declared tolerance. Persistent observables are the canonical type of observable tracked by the coercive-transport framework. They are the objects that survive from regime to regime as the refinement chain progresses, and whose transfer across regime boundaries is controlled by the unified coercive-transport inequality.
