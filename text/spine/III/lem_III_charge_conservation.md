Charge Conservation Lemma [U]: conserved charges are exactly preserved under admissible transfer maps. If Q is a conserved charge and phi is an admissible transfer map, then Q(phi(omega)) = Q(omega) for all omega. Consequently, conserved charges contribute zero to the E_n term of the coercive-transport inequality. This lemma is the formal basis for charge conservation in the coercive-transport framework: conservation is not an assumption but a consequence of the definition of conserved charge and the structure of the transfer maps.
