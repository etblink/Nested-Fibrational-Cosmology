Structural Entropy Monotonicity: the structural entropy H_n = log|Sigma_n| is non-increasing along the refinement chain: H_{n+1} <= H_n. This follows from the fact that each refinement step can only identify (never split) equivalence classes. Consequence: structural entropy is a monotone decreasing invariant; it decreases from H_0 = log|Omega| to H_inf = log|Sigma_inf|. This theorem pairs with the defect ledger via the Ledger-Entropy Duality (thm:I.ledger-entropy-duality).
