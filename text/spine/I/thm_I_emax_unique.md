Admissible-Class Canonicity: given a finite relational substrate (Omega, L) with finite compression space |Sigma_inf| < inf, the maximal admissible extension E_max is unique. No other admissible extension produces a strictly finer observational quotient. This theorem establishes that the observational structure of Book I is not a choice — there is a unique maximal admissible extension that determines Sigma_n. It is the formal ground for the no-presentation-surplus rule: presentation-level distinctions collapsed by Sigma_n have no canonical force because they are invisible to the unique maximal admissible extension.
