Finite Stabilization Theorem: every refinement chain (T_n)_{n>=0} on a finite configuration space (|Omega| < inf) stabilizes after finitely many steps. There exists N such that Sigma_n = Sigma_N =: Sigma_inf for all n >= N. The stabilization stage N is bounded by |Omega|. This is one of the two primary unconditional exports of Book I (the other being the observational quotient well-definedness). It is the formal ground for all downstream references to "the stabilized observational quotient" and "the limiting state space Sigma_inf."
