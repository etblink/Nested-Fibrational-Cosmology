A continuum bridge is a separately proved theorem certifying that a discrete result on a scoped certificate converges to a declared continuum target. It must be separately proved — it cannot be the same proof as the scoped certificate. The bridge has four components: (1) a source scoped certificate establishing the discrete claim; (2) a convergence proof showing the discrete result approaches the continuum target as the scale parameter tends to zero; (3) a target continuum object that the bridge produces; (4) a faithfulness condition ensuring the target correctly encodes the source structure. Together, the scoped certificate and the continuum bridge jointly license the promoted continuum claim (thm:VI.continuum-bridge-schema).
