A certified continuum interface regime licenses notations only within its declared scope. The scope is the declared regime S in the four-component structure (C, S, F, R). Continuum language licensed within S cannot be exported to a wider regime without a new bridge. This standing rule is the Book VI analogue of sr:II.no-local-smuggling: just as Book II prohibits local observable facts from being imported outside the collar structure, Book VI prohibits certified continuum notations from being used outside the declared interface regime.
