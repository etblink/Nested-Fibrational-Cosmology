The scale-normalized increment delta_h f(x) = (f(x+h) - f(x)) / h is the canonical discrete approximation to the derivative. It is defined for a certified observable family f and scale parameter h from the declared interface regime. The scale normalization is required to make the increment dimensionally consistent across regimes. The canonical limit lim_{h->0} delta_h f(x) = (df/dx)(x) is what Book VI calls the "effective smooth representative" of the derivative — it is not a literal limit of real-number quantities but the asymptotic encoding of the increment behavior in the certified discrete dynamics.
