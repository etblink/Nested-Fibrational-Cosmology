# **3. Global No-Go Tests**

We now formulate a set of global no-go tests designed to probe whether Nested Fibrational Cosmology can satisfy basic internal requirements simultaneously. These tests are not model-specific and do not depend on detailed parameter choices. They apply uniformly to all dynamically viable configurations admitted by the assumptions of Section 2.

Each test is framed as a **necessary condition** for a class of structural ambitions often implicitly associated with foundational frameworks. Failure of any test does not require interpretation or mitigation; it constitutes a definitive obstruction under the rules already fixed.

---

## **3.1 Existence No-Go: Compatibility Versus Nontrivial Nesting**

The first test concerns the coexistence of nontrivial nesting with global compatibility.

**Test E1 (Existence):**
Does there exist at least one dynamically viable NFC configuration in which nontrivial nested structure persists while all inter-layer compatibility conditions are satisfied globally?

This test targets a fundamental tension in the framework. Nontrivial nesting requires that distinct layers introduce genuinely new structure rather than trivial repetition. Compatibility, however, enforces coherence across the hierarchy, constraining how each layer may embed relative to others.

If compatibility conditions force nested layers to collapse into effectively equivalent structures, then nontrivial nesting fails to exist dynamically. Conversely, if nontrivial nesting violates compatibility at any depth, no globally consistent configuration exists.

**Failure condition:**
If all dynamically viable configurations either trivialize nesting or violate compatibility, NFC admits no nontrivial realizations and fails at the level of existence.

---

## **3.2 Stability No-Go: Persistence Versus Structural Richness**

The second test concerns the tension between dynamical stability and structural richness.

**Test S1 (Stability):**
Do there exist configurations that are both dynamically stable and structurally non-degenerate across multiple fibrational depths?

Paper IV established that stability acts as a hard filter, eliminating large classes of statically consistent configurations. This test asks whether anything structurally interesting survives that filter.

Structural richness here means more than the presence of multiple layers; it requires that deeper layers contribute distinct geometric or organizational features rather than being dynamically inert or eliminated entirely.

**Failure condition:**
If all dynamically stable configurations are structurally degenerate‚Äîeither collapsing layers, suppressing all deeper structure, or reducing effectively to a single-layer description‚Äîthen NFC is dynamically viable only in a trivial sense and fails to support its core architectural claim.

---

## **3.3 Emergence No-Go: Stability Versus Effective Description**

The third test targets the existence of any well-defined emergent description.

**Test M1 (Emergence):**
Does there exist at least one dynamically viable NFC configuration that admits a finite, persistent effective low-energy description?

This test formalizes a minimal requirement for physical relevance without invoking phenomenology. An effective description exists if a bounded set of collective degrees of freedom persists under evolution and remains insensitive to arbitrarily fine fibrational detail.

Paper V showed that many effective behaviors are excluded by stability and nesting. This test asks whether *any* effective description survives at all.

**Failure condition:**
If all dynamically viable configurations either exhibit unbounded mode proliferation or eliminate all collective degrees of freedom through suppression, then NFC admits no effective description and fails at the emergence level.

---

## **3.4 Decoupling No-Go: Hierarchy Versus Coherence**

The fourth test examines whether hierarchy and global coherence can coexist.

**Test D1 (Decoupling):**
Can hierarchical suppression of deeper layers occur without destroying global coherence of the nested structure?

Hierarchical decoupling is required for effective descriptions, but excessive suppression risks rendering deeper layers dynamically irrelevant. This test probes whether decoupling can be strong enough to permit emergence while weak enough to preserve the integrity of the nested architecture.

**Failure condition:**
If all configurations exhibiting effective decoupling also lose coherent nested structure‚Äîor if all coherent nested configurations exhibit runaway coupling‚Äîthen NFC cannot simultaneously support hierarchy and coherence.

---

## **3.5 Combined No-Go: Joint Satisfaction**

The final test is the most severe.

**Test C1 (Joint Viability):**
Does there exist any configuration that simultaneously satisfies Tests E1, S1, M1, and D1?

This test determines whether NFC admits *any* configuration that is:

* nontrivially nested,
* dynamically stable,
* structurally rich,

**Failure condition:**
If no configuration satisfies all four criteria simultaneously, NFC fails as a framework capable of underwriting even minimal effective physical structure.

# **4. Structural Incompatibilities**

We now examine specific incompatibilities that arise when the requirements articulated in Section 3 are imposed simultaneously on Nested Fibrational Cosmology. These incompatibilities are not contingent on parameter choices or modeling details; they follow directly from the interaction of nesting, compatibility, stability, and emergence under the frozen assumptions of Section 2.

Each subsection below isolates a distinct obstruction. Any one of them, if unavoidable, is sufficient to rule out broad classes of NFC realizations.

---

## **4.1 Nesting Versus Global Compatibility**

Nontrivial nesting requires that successive fibrational layers introduce genuinely new geometric or organizational structure. Global compatibility, however, constrains how each layer embeds within its parent and interacts with adjacent layers. These constraints are cumulative: compatibility must be satisfied not only locally but across the entire hierarchy.

This produces a fundamental tension. As nesting depth increases, compatibility conditions increasingly restrict allowable embeddings. In many cases, enforcing compatibility forces nested layers into geometrically equivalent or dynamically suppressed configurations, effectively collapsing the hierarchy into a trivial repetition of a single structure.

If compatibility constraints eliminate all nontrivial nested embeddings beyond a finite depth, NFC fails Test E1. The framework would then admit at most shallow or degenerate nesting, undermining its defining architectural claim.

---

## **4.2 Stability Versus Deep Structural Contribution**

Dynamical stability further exacerbates this tension. As shown in Paper IV, stability eliminates configurations with strong inter-layer coupling, resonant mode transfer, or geometric fragility. Deeper layers are therefore subject to increasingly stringent suppression if the configuration is to remain viable under evolution.

This leads to a second incompatibility. For deeper layers to contribute meaningfully to the structure, they must influence the dynamics or organization of the configuration. Yet any such influence risks destabilizing the system unless it is tightly constrained or suppressed.

If all dynamically stable configurations suppress deep-layer influence to the point of effective irrelevance, NFC fails Test S1. The framework would then support stability only by sacrificing structural richness, reducing to a dynamically viable but architecturally empty form.

---

## **4.3 Emergence Versus Structural Persistence**

Effective emergence requires that a finite set of collective degrees of freedom persists under coarse-graining and evolution. In NFC, this persistence depends on hierarchical decoupling: deeper layers must not destabilize the effective description, but neither can they be eliminated entirely if the nested architecture is to remain meaningful.

This creates a narrow window. If decoupling is too weak, effective descriptions fail to exist due to uncontrolled mode proliferation. If decoupling is too strong, deeper layers become dynamically inert, and the nested structure ceases to play any role in the emergent description.

If no dynamically viable configuration lies within this window, NFC fails Test M1. In that case, the framework admits either no effective descriptions or only trivial ones that do not reflect the nested architecture.

---

## **4.4 Hierarchy Versus Coherence**

Hierarchical suppression is essential for emergence, but it threatens global coherence. Strong decoupling can isolate layers to the point that the nested structure no longer functions as a coherent whole. Conversely, maintaining coherence across layers increases coupling and risks destabilization.

This incompatibility targets Test D1. If coherence requires coupling strengths that destabilize the configuration, while decoupling destroys coherence, NFC cannot support both hierarchy and global structural integrity simultaneously.

Such a result would not merely limit the framework; it would undermine its central organizing principle.

---

## **4.5 Joint Incompatibility and Cumulative Obstruction**

The most severe obstruction arises when these incompatibilities reinforce one another.
